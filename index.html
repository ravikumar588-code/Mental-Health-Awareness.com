About the Topic

Mental health shapes how we think, feel, learn, and connect.

Mental health includes emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects confidence, relationships, academic performance, productivity, and physical health.

Selected Signal

Headache or Body Pain

Signs: Frequent headaches, body pain, muscle tightness, or tiredness.

Possible reason: Stress can keep the body in a tense state and reduce rest quality.

Care step: Drink water, rest your eyes, stretch gently, and take a short break from screens.

Mental Health Knowledge Hub

Useful information students and community members can remember and apply.

01

When should someone seek help?

If stress, sadness, fear, sleep problems, or low energy continue for many days and affect daily life, support from family, teachers, counselors, or health professionals is important.

02

What can friends do?

Friends can listen calmly, avoid judging, stay connected, encourage healthy routines, and guide the person toward trusted adults or professional help when needed.

03

Why is stigma harmful?

Stigma makes people hide their problems. Awareness helps communities treat mental health with the same seriousness and care as physical health.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Method

Notice 5 things you can see, 4 things you can feel, 3 sounds you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste. This can calm racing thoughts.

Positive Self-Talk Examples

Use simple lines such as “I can take one step at a time,” “This feeling will pass,” and “It is okay to ask for support.”

Healthy Study-Life Balance

Plan small study sessions, take short breaks, sleep regularly, reduce late-night screen use, and include movement or outdoor time each day.

How to Start a Supportive Conversation

Begin with care: “I noticed you seem stressed. Do you want to talk?” Listen first, avoid quick advice, and encourage help if the concern feels serious.
